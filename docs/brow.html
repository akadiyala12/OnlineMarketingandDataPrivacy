Browser Fingerprinting

What is browser fingerprinting?

Websites use monitoring techniques called browser fingerprinting (also known as computer fingerprinting or online fingerprinting) to gather information about you. Scripts, which are collections of instructions that tell your browser what to do, are needed for modern website functions.

Scripts will recognise a lot of details about your computer and browser in the background and stitch it together to form your unique online "fingerprint." This fingerprint can then be used to track you down across the internet and across various browsing sessions.

Since websites use scripts that run in the background of your browser, browser fingerprinting is possible. APIs are built-in software functions in today's web browsers that can be used by website scripts to collect data.

To conduct cross-site monitoring, many website owners and ad networks share browser fingerprinting features. That means they monitor you around the web and gather personal information about you, such as your browsing history, shopping and news habits, and so on.

How to prevent browser fingerprinting

Browser fingerprinting is highly difficult to prevent without sophisticated methods. Browser fingerprinting can't be avoided by using privacy features like private browsing or Incognito mode, clearing the cookies or search history, or using an ad blocker or VPN.
