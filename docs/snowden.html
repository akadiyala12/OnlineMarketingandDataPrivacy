Snowden

Who is Edward Snowden?

Edward Snowden is a US citizen, former Intelligence Community officer, and whistleblower who is 31 years old. The documents he published with the help of others provided a critical public insight into the NSA's classified mass surveillance activities and capabilities, as well as those of its foreign intelligence partners. These disclosures drew unprecedented attention to privacy invasions and digital protection around the world, sparking a global debate on the topic.

What did he do?

Snowden served in the US Intelligence Community in a variety of capacities, including as a CIA undercover agent in other countries. When he left his home and family in Hawaii to blow the whistle in May 2013, he was working as an infrastructure analyst at the NSA on a Booz Allen Hamilton contract.

Following his trip to Hong Kong, Snowden released documents to the American public outlining the NSA's mass surveillance activities, which were discovered to be functioning without any public scrutiny and beyond the US Constitution's boundaries.

Snowden has been charged with theft of government property and two additional counts of treason under the 1917 Espionage Act by the US government. Each charge carries a maximum penalty of ten years in prison.

Where is he now?

With the US seeking his extradition, Snowden has relocated to Russia, where he has been granted a three-year residency beginning August 1, 2014, following a year of temporary asylum that ended on July 31, 2014.

He has since then been living with his wife Lindsay in Russia and the two are expecting a baby. He even mentioned they were applying for Russian citizenship. Journalists continue to publish documents from Edward Snowden that expose the covert and unaccountable surveillance programs that exist today.
